## What it is

A one-page pitch site built for a single company - FirstClub, a Bengaluru-based premium quick-commerce startup that closed a $55M Series B in May 2026. The page lives at [sagarahuja.co/firstclub](/firstclub) and is the application artefact, not a CV attached to it.

## The setup

- A one-page pitch styled as FirstClub's own app's product page. Same green, same Playfair Display, same cream background.
- A 12-month outsider's thesis on the four problems they need to solve to scale from 170K to 1M households. Discovery and trust, multi-city quality, personalisation, membership.
- A mobile-first showcase wrapped in an iPhone bezel. The phone pins as you scroll, the content reveals inside, then releases.
- PostHog wired in. The dashboard tells me which of the four problems get read, which get skipped, and whether the CTA at the bottom gets clicked.
- A specific ask at the bottom: 20 minutes.

## Built with

Astro for the site. Claude for the pair-programming. GitHub Pages for hosting. PostHog free tier for analytics. End-to-end cost so far is zero.

## Why this format

A normal application has the company doing all the inference. They read a CV, guess at what you might know, guess at what you might solve, guess at whether you might fit. The work of imagining you in the role is theirs.

A pitch page flips that. You do the work of imagining yourself in the role, and you show them. The four problems I would prioritise in twelve months. The bets I would make. The metrics I would track. The infrastructure I would build to scale from one city to five.

The conversation that starts after a pitch page is different. It does not start with "tell me about yourself". It starts with "you said X about discovery, I disagree, here is why".

That is the conversation I want to have.
