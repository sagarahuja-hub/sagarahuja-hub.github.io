The Problem

Parents want to expose children to as many extracurriculars as possible - but the current model is slow, narrow, and penalises early exit.

01
Sequential discovery - one activity at a time, months per cycle
02
Limited range - parents default to the same 5-6 familiar activities
03
Sunk cost lock-in - paid enrollment discourages early exit
04
Hidden callings - many passions are never found because the activity was never tried, or never even considered
The Idea

A child attends the center up to five days a week. Each day is a different activity - one hour per session. Once a milestone threshold is reached per discipline, the child deepens it or rotates. All under one enrollment.

Key Pillars
Expert instructors per discipline
Each activity taught by a qualified specialist. Children receive a genuine experience from day one.
In-house career coach
Tracks engagement, surfaces early signals of aptitude, and guides when to deepen or move on.
Single enrollment, one fee
No juggling separate institutes, schedules, or payments. One center handles everything.
Milestone-based progression
Advancement tracked by structured milestones per activity - not just hours clocked.
The Hidden Opportunity Layer

Most parents draw from a small set of familiar activities. Many of the highest-potential disciplines never enter their consideration set - not because of disinterest, but because of low visibility.

These are precisely the activities where talent pools are thinnest, competition is structurally lower, and profile differentiation is highest.

Why Bengaluru, Why Now

International board schools (IB, IGCSE, Cambridge) are growing rapidly - Bengaluru ranks among the fastest-growing hubs in India. These curricula place explicit weight on extracurricular portfolios and holistic development.

Parents in these schools are already primed to invest in structured extracurricular exploration - making this a reachable, motivated audience without needing to build the behaviour from scratch.
